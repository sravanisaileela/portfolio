About me

I have always been curious and fascinated by the stories that architecture tells. Growing up

in the historic city of Hyderabad, I was surrounded by layers of culture, craft, and history that

shaped how I see the built environment . It is something living, evolving, deeply human and

very important part of our lived experience. India’s ever-changing relationship with its

heritage taught me that preservation is not about freezing time, but about guiding change

with respect and imagination.

Now, living and studying in London, I find myself inspired by another city rich in architectural

dialogue, a place where old and new coexist in harmony. My work explores how research,

critique, thoughtful planning and design can protect what makes a place special, while

allowing it to grow and adapt for the future. I’m passionate about bridging history and

modernity, and creating spaces that celebrate both memory and progress.

I’m Sravani and I love exploring the journey between ancient streets to modern skylines.
