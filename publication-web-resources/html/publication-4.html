How can the integration of community-led commoning initiatives in retrofitting and sustainable practices influence resident agency and social cohesion in urban neighborhoods facing issues of social isola- tion and climate change?

‘Common Threads’ aims to address social isolation and stigma in Somer’s Town by revitalising the community’s physical and visual commons. Situated in Oak- shott Court, the project focuses on enhancing community well-being through co-designed initiatives, energy-efficient retrofitting, and sustainable practices. By employing a carefully staged econimic strategy, it begins with grassroots efforts and progressively engages larger institutions like Camden Council, en- suring that funding does not solely rely on these entities that often lack funds to deliver genuine results.

A crucial aspect of the project is empowering residents, fostering a sense of agency, and encouraging stewardship towards community-driven changes. This is achieved through initiatives that promote resident engagement and collaboration by working with climate funds and waste reduction, allowing the community to take ownership of their environment.

The project also leverages the Somers Town Decentralised Energy Network (DEN) while exploring local energy suppliers to enhance sustainability.To mitigate increasing estate heat risk and loss, the initiative integrates new technologies, such as creating open public spaces with tensile textiles made from recycled plastic waste, which help reduce energy consumption and cost for residents.

This approach not only ad- dresses immediate climate challenges but also serves as a model for future projects in the area and beyond. By alleviating the financial pressure on large institutions and placing agency into the hands of residents, the initiative fosters a community that revives it’s social, environmental and economical energies.
